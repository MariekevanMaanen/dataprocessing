Scatterplot about PM2.5 exposure

Marieke van Maanen (10888640)

This plot visualizes PM2.5 exposure against population density per country.
PM2.5 (Dutch: fijnstof) refers to atmospheric particulate matter (PM) that have a diameter of less than 2.5 micrometers.
Fine particles can come from various sources like motor vehicles which are more common in densenly populated areas.
The third variable represents % of renewable electricity used per country which contains less PM2.5 than other electricity sources.
